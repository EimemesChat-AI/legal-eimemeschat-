About Us

"Preserving language is preserving identity" — this principle guides our work.

Background

Indigenous and minority language communities have historically been underserved by technological advancement. While global platforms have expanded rapidly, languages of Northeast India — including Thadou‑Kuki and related dialects — have seen limited representation in digital spaces. EimemesChat was developed to address this gap, ensuring that as artificial intelligence becomes ubiquitous, our communities have a presence and a voice.

Mission

To develop accessible, AI‑powered tools that serve indigenous language communities, creating technology that represents and preserves cultural identity. We maintain that language constitutes a fundamental component of identity, and that every community deserves to participate in the digital age on its own terms.

Technology

EimemesChat AI is powered by state‑of‑the‑art large language models accessed through Groq and Google (Gemini). The client application is open‑source — built with React, Firebase, and Vercel — and is actively maintained by a solo developer. We combine modern web technologies with a deep respect for cultural heritage.

Language Development

Technology for Thadou‑Kuki language processing is an active area of development. The technical challenge lies not in the impossibility of the task, but in the focused effort required to adapt existing models and frameworks to our specific linguistic context. We are actively working toward implementing native Kuki language capabilities and welcome collaboration from linguists, speakers, and technologists who share this objective.

Vision

Human language is the most powerful force in the AI era — the right words now accomplish what once required deep expertise. Yet low‑resource languages like Thadou‑Kuki remain on the margins, deserving not just preservation but a place at the forefront. Our vision is a platform where Kuki speakers engage with AI in their mother tongue, natively and seamlessly, because in the age of AI, language is the ultimate interface, and every language deserves its place.

Development & Maintenance

EimemesChat is an independently developed project, built and maintained without corporate backing or external funding. This independence allows us to prioritize cultural preservation, user privacy, and accessibility over commercial objectives. Every aspect — from frontend design to backend infrastructure to legal compliance — is handled by a single developer committed to the mission.

Operational Costs

Maintaining a free service requires ongoing resources:

  • AI API costs — Each conversation incurs computational costs with our model providers that scale with usage.
  • Infrastructure & hosting — Firebase, Vercel, and related services ensuring reliable 24/7 availability.
  • Development — Continuous improvement, bug fixes, security updates, and feature development.
  • Research — Advancing Thadou‑Kuki language integration and cultural preservation.

Development History

The application evolved from a simple prototype through multiple architecture iterations, provider integrations (Groq, Gemini), and extensive stability enhancements. Each update — whether visual refinement, performance optimization, or new feature — represents an ongoing commitment to quality and user experience. The app has grown from a basic chat interface to a full‑featured AI assistant with streaming responses, file support, web search, voice capabilities, and robust security measures.

Roadmap

  • Current phase (v4.x): Refining user experience, improving reliability, and building community.
  • Near‑term: Implementing native Thadou‑Kuki language capabilities.
  • Long‑term: Expanding to support additional indigenous languages of Northeast India and beyond.

Transparency

We maintain openness about our capabilities and limitations. The path to comprehensive linguistic representation requires sustained effort and resources. We offer no immediate solutions, only sustained commitment to the communities we serve. This project is a long‑term endeavour, not a short‑term experiment.
